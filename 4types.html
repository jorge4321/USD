What is Innovation?

It is not just "invention". It is the process of creating value by applying novel solutions to meaningful problems.

The Innovation Matrix

We classify innovation based on two dimensions: Technology and Market.

The 4 Types Overview

1. Incremental Innovation

Existing Technology + Existing Market

Small improvements to existing products. Low risk, but necessary to stay competitive.

Example: Smartphones

The iPhone 14 vs iPhone 15. Better camera, faster chip, same core function.

Incremental: The Reality

Most companies spend 80% of their budget here because it is predictable.

Risk Level
Frequency

2. Architectural Innovation

Existing Technology + New Market

Taking existing components or technology and reconfiguring them for a new use or market.

Example: Ride Sharing

Uber used existing tech (GPS, Smartphones, 4G) to create a completely new service market.

Reconfiguration

It's like moving a plant from a forest to a greenhouse. Same plant, new environment, massive growth.

3. Disruptive Innovation

New Technology + Existing Market

Often starts as inferior or cheaper technology, then overtakes the market leaders.

The Curve

Disruptive tech starts at the bottom (low quality/cheap) and improves faster than incumbents expect.

Example: Netflix vs Blockbuster

Streaming started with poor quality and limited selection. Now it dominates.

4. Radical Innovation

New Technology + New Market

The "Breakthrough". Creates industries that didn't exist before using revolutionary tech.

Example: The Internet / Airplane

High uncertainty, high risk, but potentially infinite reward.

High Risk, High Reward

A balanced portfolio often includes a mix of all four types.
